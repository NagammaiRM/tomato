The tomato, originally domesticated in the Andes Mountains of Peru and Mexico, became one of the most important crops exchanged during the Columbian Exchange. After Spanish conquistadors encountered this vibrant fruit in the 16th century, it spread across Europe, Asia, and Africa, transforming cuisines and agriculture worldwide. Today, tomatoes are integral to Italian, Spanish, Indian, and countless other culinary traditions.
