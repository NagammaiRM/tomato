The tomato's journey from the Andes Mountains to tables worldwide is one of history's most remarkable agricultural exchanges. Originally domesticated in Peru and Ecuador, tomatoes traveled to Mesoamerica before Spanish conquistadors brought them to Europe in the early 1500s. From there, they spread across continents through trade routes, transforming global cuisine and becoming essential to Italian pasta sauces, Indian curries, and countless other dishes.
